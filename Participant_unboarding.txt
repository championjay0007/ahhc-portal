# AHHC PORTAL ONBOARDING GUIDE

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 8 July 2026
**Purpose:** This guide explains the full onboarding journey for participants and workers in the AHHC Portal, starting from the participant enquiry form, including how participants nominate workers and how AHHC Administrators respond at each stage.

---

# 1. Introduction
The AHHC Portal supports two related onboarding journeys:

- Participant onboarding – for individuals seeking services through AHHC.
- Worker onboarding – for workers, support providers, or suppliers who will provide services to participants.

Both journeys are designed to:

- protect participant information,
- collect required information and documentation,
- ensure compliance with AHHC policies,
- require administrative review before access is granted,
- maintain a full audit trail of onboarding actions.

No participant or worker receives full portal access until the required approval steps have been completed by an AHHC Administrator.

---

# 2. Participant Onboarding Journey: Starting from the Enquiry Form
The participant onboarding journey begins when a person submits an enquiry form through the AHHC Portal or an approved intake channel.

## Step 1 – Enquiry Form Submitted
When a participant submits an enquiry form, the system records:

- the participant’s contact details,
- basic service request information,
- the date and time of the enquiry,
- the intake source or referral channel.

This stage creates the initial participant record and begins the onboarding process.

## Step 2 – Enquiry Review by AHHC Administration
An AHHC Administrator reviews the submitted enquiry.

The administrator may:

- confirm the enquiry is valid,
- request more information,
- assign the enquiry to the appropriate service team,
- move the participant forward to onboarding.

If the enquiry is accepted, the participant is invited into the onboarding process.

## Step 3 – Invitation and Access
Once the enquiry is accepted, the participant receives an onboarding invitation.

The invitation typically includes:

- a secure onboarding link,
- the participant’s email address,
- instructions to begin the onboarding process.

The participant can:

- start the onboarding wizard,
- save progress and return later,
- complete the process in stages.

---

# 3. Participant Onboarding Steps
After the invitation is accepted, the participant completes the onboarding process through the guided workflow.

## Step 1 – Account Setup
The participant creates or confirms their portal account by:

- setting a password,
- confirming login credentials,
- completing MFA if enabled.

## Step 2 – Personal Profile Information
The participant enters their personal details, including:

- full name,
- preferred name,
- date of birth,
- contact details,
- address,
- preferred communication method.

## Step 3 – Emergency Contact Information
The participant provides emergency contact details such as:

- name,
- relationship,
- phone number,
- email address.

## Step 4 – Support Person Information
The participant may nominate support persons, such as:

- family members,
- guardians,
- advocates,
- carers,
- support coordinators,
- legal representatives.

These people may be recorded as support contacts for the participant’s care and service planning.

## Step 5 – Document Upload
The participant uploads required documents, such as:

- identification,
- referral documents,
- medical or support-related documentation,
- other evidence requested by AHHC.

## Step 6 – Agreements and Electronic Signatures
The participant reviews and signs required agreements, including:

- privacy consent,
- service agreements,
- declarations,
- terms and conditions.

## Step 7 – Review and Submission
Before submission, the participant reviews all entered information and confirms that required documents and agreements are complete.

Once submitted:

- the onboarding becomes read-only,
- the submission is routed to AHHC Administration,
- the participant status changes to Pending Admin Review.

---

# 4. How Participants Nominate Workers
Participants can nominate workers as part of their support arrangement or service planning.

## Nomination Process
A participant may nominate a worker when:

- they want a specific worker to assist them,
- they are requesting a worker from a preferred provider,
- they are selecting support staff for their care plan.

The nomination typically includes:

- the worker’s name or registration details,
- the service requested,
- the participant’s reason for the nomination,
- any relevant support notes.

## What Happens After Nomination
Once a worker is nominated:

- the nomination is recorded against the participant’s profile,
- AHHC Administration reviews the nomination,
- the nominated worker is checked against eligibility and compliance requirements,
- the worker may be approved or declined for the participant’s services.

This ensures that nominated workers are appropriate, verified, and authorised before they begin delivering services.

---

# 5. Administrator Review of Participant Onboarding
When participant onboarding is submitted, the administrator reviews the full submission.

## Review Tasks
The administrator verifies:

- personal information,
- contact details,
- emergency contacts,
- support person nominations,
- uploaded documents,
- signed agreements.

## Possible Decisions
The administrator may:

- approve the participant,
- request changes,
- reject the submission.

## Notification to Participant
The participant is notified of the outcome through the portal and/or email.

Possible statuses include:

- Pending Review,
- Changes Requested,
- Approved,
- Rejected.

---

# 6. Worker Onboarding Workflow
The worker onboarding workflow is separate from the participant process, but closely connected when a participant nominates or is assigned a worker.

## Stage 1 – Invitation and Account Creation
A worker receives an invitation from AHHC Administration and creates their account.

## Stage 2 – Compliance Documentation Upload
The worker submits required documents, including:

- police checks,
- qualifications,
- licenses,
- insurance certificates,
- other compliance evidence.

## Stage 3 – Compliance Review
AHHC Administration reviews the worker’s uploaded documents.

## Stage 4 – Declarations and Agreements
The worker signs required declarations and agreements.

## Stage 5 – Service Approval
The administrator approves the services the worker may deliver.

## Stage 6 – Participant Assignment
Once approved, the worker may be assigned to one or more participants, depending on eligibility and service approval.

---

# 7. How the Two Workflows Connect
The participant and worker onboarding journeys connect in the following way:

- a participant enters the system through the enquiry form and onboarding process,
- the participant may nominate a worker,
- AHHC Administration reviews the nomination and the worker’s compliance status,
- if approved, the worker can be assigned to support the participant.

This creates a controlled, compliant, and auditable process for service delivery.

---

# 8. Administrator Responsibilities
Throughout both onboarding workflows, AHHC Administrators are responsible for:

- reviewing enquiries and submissions,
- validating documents,
- checking worker compliance,
- reviewing participant nominations,
- requesting corrections where necessary,
- approving or rejecting submissions,
- assigning workers to participants,
- maintaining audit records.

---

# 9. Key Principles
The AHHC onboarding process is designed to ensure:

- secure intake and registration,
- accurate participant records,
- verified worker credentials,
- compliant service delivery,
- controlled access to sensitive information,
- clear accountability and auditability.

---

# 10. Conclusion
The AHHC Portal onboarding process begins when a participant submits an enquiry form and continues through participant registration, documentation, review, and approval. Participants may also nominate workers, and those nominations are reviewed by AHHC Administration before a worker is approved and assigned. This structure ensures that services are delivered safely, lawfully, and in a well-managed way.

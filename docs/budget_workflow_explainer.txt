Hey — Sam here. I'll walk you through how the budget system works from both the Admin side and the Participant side, step-by-step, like I'm explaining it to a colleague.

ADMIN: what you do and see
- Where to go:
  1. Log in to the Control Center (Admin Portal).
  2. Open "Budget & Finance" → "Budgets".
  3. You can also open a specific participant record and click the "Create Budget" button there to prefill the participant.

- Create a budget:
  - Click "Create Budget".
  - Fill the form fields: Participant (pre-filled if launched from a participant page), Quarter Start Date, Quarter End Date, Opening Budget (dollars), Carry Over (dollars).
  - Submit the form.
  - What happens behind the scenes: a new row is written to the `budgets` table keyed to the `participant_id` and quarter dates. The app stores money values in cents (e.g., $1,000 → 100000 cents) and the `BudgetService` computes canonical metrics.

- What you see in the Budgets list:
  - Columns: Participant, Quarter (e.g., 2026-07-01 → 2026-09-30), Total Available, Committed, Remaining, Actions (View/Edit).
  - "Total Available" = Opening Budget + Carry Over.
  - "Committed" = funds currently reserved/committed (pre-approvals, pending invoices, etc.).
  - "Remaining" = funds left to be committed/used (Total Available minus any committed/paid amounts).

- View a budget detail:
  - Click "View" on a budget row.
  - You’ll see a ledger: transactions, commits/releases, pre-approvals and invoice history, and derived totals.
  - Admins can perform actions: commit funds, release commitments, mark spends as paid, or edit/delete the budget per permissions.

- Who controls the numbers:
  - Admin sets the Opening Budget and Carry Over (these establish the quarter’s funds).
  - Pre-approvals and invoices do not change the opening balance — they change committed/approved/paid metrics.
  - `BudgetService` handles calculations and ensures the portal shows consistent canonical metrics.

- Permissions and behavior notes:
  - Only users with admin role can see all budgets. Regular users are restricted to their own participant budgets.
  - The system uses policies to gate actions (we fixed a role-check issue so policy calls behave correctly).

PARTICIPANT: what they do and see
- Where to go:
  1. Log in to the Participant Portal.
  2. Open "My Budget" from the sidebar.

- What the participant sees:
  - The same canonical metrics but scoped to the participant: Quarter range, Total Available, Committed, Remaining.
  - A simplified ledger showing pre-approvals and submitted invoices, and whether they’re approved/committed/paid.
  - Participants cannot create budgets — admins create budgets for participants.

- Actions available to the participant:
  - Submit pre-approval requests (these may reserve funds by increasing "Committed").
  - Submit invoices against services; invoice approvals update approved/paid metrics and affect remaining balance.

- How to read the numbers (simple example):
  - Admin creates a budget: Opening $1,000, Carry Over $200 → Total Available $1,200.
  - Participant submits a pre-approval that reserves $100 → Committed $100, Remaining $1,100.
  - An invoice for $100 is marked paid → Paid increases and Remaining decreases accordingly.

EXTRA: common navigation flows
- Admin creates a budget for a participant:
  1. Admin opens Participant → clicks "Create Budget" → form pre-fills participant → submits.
  2. App creates the budget row and redirects back to Budgets list or the created budget page.
  3. Admin sees Total Available in the list (Opening + Carry Over).

- Admin reviews budget usage:
  1. From Budgets list, click "View" → inspect transactions and commitments.
  2. Approve or reject pre-approvals, or mark invoices as paid to update metrics.

TROUBLESHOOTING pointers (short):
- If the Budgets page errors on load, check policy role checks and column names (e.g., `quarter_start_date` vs `quarter_start`).
- If numbers look wrong, confirm that the budget row exists for the corre
ct participant & quarter and that `BudgetService` is being used to compute metrics.

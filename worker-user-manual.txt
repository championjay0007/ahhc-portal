AHHC Portal Worker User Manual and Onboarding Guide

This manual explains how workers navigate the AHHC Portal, manage assignments, and complete onboarding steps.

1. WORKER PORTAL OVERVIEW

Workers use the portal to manage assigned participants, shifts, care notes, incidents, and documents.

Primary sections:
- Dashboard: Worker homepage with assignments, tasks, and status.
- Assigned Participants: Review participants assigned to the worker and access participant details.
- Shifts: Confirm, start, and complete shifts.
- Care Notes: Create care notes and record visit details.
- Incidents: Report incidents and follow up on issues.
- Documents: Upload and download forms and participant records.
- Invoices and Forms: Access invoicing items and documents for review or signature.

2. NAVIGATING THE WORKER PORTAL

Dashboard:
- The worker dashboard opens after login.
- Review assigned tasks and quick links to active participants and shifts.
- The dashboard typically includes:
  - An overview of today’s shifts and whether they are confirmed, in progress, or completed.
  - A list of assigned participants and any new care notes or incident tasks for them.
  - Alerts for urgent items such as unreported incidents, overdue notes, or new assignment changes.
  - Budget and authorization summaries for assigned participants, if the portal supports these financial details.
  - Pending preapproval requests for supplies, equipment, or care updates that affect your assignments.
  - Quick links to start or complete shifts, add care notes, and access participant details.
- Use the dashboard for a fast summary of your daily responsibilities and to prioritize work.

Assigned Participants:
- Click Assigned Participants to see the list of people you care for.
- Open a participant record for details, history, and notes.

Shifts:
- Open Shifts to view your schedule.
- Confirm an upcoming shift to accept it.
- Start a shift when you arrive and complete it when your work is finished.
- Use shift notes to record important handover information.

Care Notes:
- Use Care Notes to document observations and care activity.
- Select the participant and create a new note with the required details.
- Save the note to maintain a record of your visit.

Incidents:
- Report incidents promptly through the Incidents section.
- Complete all required fields and include important context.
- Follow any incident management guidance from your supervisor.

Budget and Preapprovals:
- Review the participant budget and authorization details if your role includes care plan or financial oversight.
- Track preapproval requests for services, equipment, or care changes that require admin authorization.
- Enter the correct service code, cost estimate, and justification when submitting a request.
- Check whether preapproval requests are pending, approved, or declined before proceeding with the related work.

Documents and Forms:
- Upload documents when required by your agency.
- Download forms to review details or upload completed paperwork.
- Sign forms if the portal supports worker signatures.

3. WORKER ONBOARDING MANUAL

Overview:
This onboarding section helps new workers get started with the portal and access essential tools.

Step 1: Receive login credentials
- Your manager supplies your worker account details and temporary password.
- Visit the worker login page and sign in using the provided credentials.

Step 2: Set a secure password
- After first login, reset your password if prompted.
- Choose a strong password and keep it confidential.

Step 3: Complete profile setup
- Update your contact information and personal details.
- Confirm your email and phone number if the portal requires verification.

Step 4: Review your assignments
- Open Assigned Participants to see your current roster.
- Familiarize yourself with each participant’s profile and care needs.

Step 5: Learn shift and incident workflows
- Use the Shifts section to verify your schedule.
- Practice confirming, starting, and completing shifts.
- Review Incident reporting so you can submit accurate incident records.

Step 6: Use support if needed
- If you have portal questions, use the support ticket section or contact your admin.
- Report technical issues promptly so admins can resolve them.

4. COMMON WORKER ACTIONS

- Confirm and start shifts as soon as you arrive.
- Use care notes to log tasks, observations, and participant responses.
- Report incidents immediately after they occur.
- Keep participant documents organized and upload paperwork as required.

5. BEST PRACTICES

- Always lock your session when away from the computer.
- Keep participant details and notes accurate.
- Use the portal’s support tools for technical or account issues.
- Review the worker dashboard regularly for new assignments or changes.

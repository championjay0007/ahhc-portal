AHHC Portal Participant User Manual

This manual explains how participants navigate the AHHC Portal to manage account tasks, support requests, and portal communications.

1. PARTICIPANT PORTAL OVERVIEW

Participants use the portal to manage personal account details and communicate with support staff.

Primary sections:
- Dashboard: The main landing page after login. Displays account summary and links to important actions.
- Support: Submit support tickets and track responses.
- Messages: View message conversations if direct messaging is available.
- Profile: Update personal information and contact details.

2. NAVIGATING THE PARTICIPANT PORTAL

Dashboard:
- Begin on the participant dashboard after logging in.
- Use the dashboard links to access support and messages quickly.
- The dashboard generally contains:
  - A summary of your open support tickets and their current status.
  - Recent messages or notifications from the admin team.
  - Current budget authorizations and approved care plan amounts if available.
  - Pending preapproval requests and the status of any outstanding authorizations.
  - Quick actions for creating a new ticket or viewing your ticket history.
  - Important upcoming reminders or announcements from the portal.
- From the dashboard, you can usually click a card to go directly to the relevant support ticket, request, or authorization detail.

Support section:
- Open Support or Tickets from the participant navigation.
- Create a new ticket by entering the subject, category, priority, and description.
- Review your existing tickets and any admin responses.
- Add follow-up messages when needed.

Messages section:
- If the portal offers private messaging, visit Messages to review the conversation list.
- Click a message thread to read and respond.

Profile section:
- Update personal contact details and account information.
- Verify email address and phone number if required.

3. PARTICIPANT SUPPORT WORKFLOW

Submitting support requests:
- Use the support page to open a new ticket.
- Choose the correct category and describe the issue clearly.
- Submit the ticket and wait for an admin response.

Reviewing support replies:
- Check ticket status and message history.
- Respond to follow-up questions or clarifications from admins.
- Close resolved tickets if the portal supports that action.

Budget and preapproval requests:
- Review your budget or care plan authorizations if these are displayed in your account.
- Submit preapproval requests for services, equipment, or care changes when required.
- Attach any supporting documents and include clear justification for the request.
- Check the status of preapproval requests from the dashboard or support ticket details.

4. COMMON PARTICIPANT ACTIONS

- Reset a password if you cannot log in (use the portal’s password reset flow).
- Use the Contact or support widget on the public site for general questions if not logged in.
- Protect your account by logging out after use.

5. BEST PRACTICES

- Keep your profile information up to date.
- Use clear, concise language in support tickets.
- Reference ticket numbers when asking for follow-up.
- Monitor your inbox and notifications for admin replies.
